# ContinuuAI Sales & Marketing Materials

Complete set of ready-to-use documents for customer conversations, procurement, and legal review.

## Document Overview

### 1. [Sales Call Example](01_SALES_CALL_EXAMPLE.md)
**Purpose:** Training material and conversation template  
**Audience:** Internal sales team, founders  
**Use:** Study before first customer calls

**Key Features:**
- 42-minute full transcript with CSO of 2,500-employee health system
- Demonstrates qualification approach
- Shows ethical boundary setting
- Real objection handling
- Post-call analysis included

**When to use:** Before any first customer conversation

---

### 2. [Procurement ROI Document](02_PROCUREMENT_ROI_DOCUMENT.md)
**Purpose:** Financial justification for enterprise buyers  
**Audience:** Procurement teams, CFOs, finance departments  
**Use:** Send after initial interest, before contract

**Key Features:**
- Conservative ROI analysis (2x - 42x annual investment)
- Budget source mapping (Risk/Strategy/Exec Office)
- Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) breakdown
- Measurable success criteria
- Calculation worksheet included

**When to use:** When buyer asks "how do we justify this internally?"

---

### 3. [Legal Contract Template](03_LEGAL_CONTRACT_TEMPLATE.md)
**Purpose:** Standard service agreement with ethical safeguards  
**Audience:** Customer legal teams, our legal counsel  
**Use:** Starting point for all customer contracts

**Key Features:**
- Section 1.3: Human Judgment Primacy Clause
- Section 3: Absolute customer data ownership
- Section 8.4: Ethical termination right (no penalty)
- Section 9.1: No liability cap for data breaches
- Exhibits: Subprocessors, Acceptable Use, DPA

**When to use:** After pilot approval or full contract negotiation

---

### 4. [Marketing One-Pager](04_MARKETING_ONE_PAGER.md)
**Purpose:** Executive summary for decision-makers  
**Audience:** C-suite, strategy officers, board members  
**Use:** Leave-behind after calls, email attachment

**Key Features:**
- Problem/solution in 2 paragraphs
- ROI scenarios with real numbers
- "What we refuse to build" appendix
- Case study excerpt
- FAQ section
- Implementation timeline

**When to use:** 
- After qualification call (if interested)
- For exec team presentation prep
- Conference follow-ups

---

### 5. [Founder's Promise](05_FOUNDERS_PROMISE.md)
**Purpose:** Ethical commitment and vision document  
**Audience:** Customers, partners, public  
**Use:** Build trust, demonstrate values alignment

**Key Features:**
- Permanent architectural refusals (no recommendations, scoring, nudging, etc.)
- Revenue model justification
- Accountability structure
- What failure looks like
- Personal founder commitment

**When to use:**
- When customer asks "how do we know you won't change?"
- During cultural fit discussions
- For values-driven buyers (NGOs, mission-driven orgs)

## Usage Guidelines by Sales Stage

### Stage 1: First Contact
**Documents needed:**
- None (conversation only)
- Optionally: Marketing One-Pager (section 1-2 only)

**Goal:** Qualify problem existence

---

### Stage 2: Interest Confirmed
**Send:**
- Marketing One-Pager (full document)
- Founder's Promise (if values-driven buyer)

**Goal:** Enable internal selling

---

### Stage 3: Executive Alignment
**Prepare:**
- Review Sales Call Example
- Customize Procurement ROI Document with their numbers

**Goal:** Address objections, quantify value

---

### Stage 4: Procurement Review
**Send:**
- Procurement ROI Document (customized)
- Legal Contract Template (for review)

**Goal:** Pass legal and finance approval

---

### Stage 5: Contract Negotiation
**Use:**
- Legal Contract Template as baseline
- Be firm on ethical clauses (1.3, 8.4)
- Flexible on pricing structure, SLA details

**Goal:** Close with principles intact

## Document Customization Guide

### What to Customize Per Customer

**Procurement ROI Document:**
- Replace scenario costs with customer's actual numbers
- Add customer-specific risk scenarios
- Update budget source recommendation
- Include industry comparables if available

**Legal Contract:**
- Geographic storage region (Section 3.3)
- Governing law (Section 12.1)
- Specific subprocessors (Exhibit A)
- Custom SLA requirements (Section 5)

**Marketing One-Pager:**
- Replace case study with similar industry example
- Add customer's language to problem statement
- Adjust ideal customer profile if needed

---

### What NEVER Changes

**In ALL Documents:**
- Ethical refusals (no recommendations, scoring, nudging, surveillance)
- Exit safety commitments
- Human judgment primacy
- Evidence-first approach
- Data ownership

**These are non-negotiable across all customers.**

## Common Questions

**Q: Can we send the Legal Contract before procurement asks?**  
A: Yes, but only after interest is confirmed and they've read the Procurement ROI Document. Sending it too early can create unnecessary friction.

**Q: Should we always include the Founder's Promise?**  
A: Not always. Use it when:
- Customer explicitly asks about values/ethics
- Dealing with NGOs or mission-driven orgs
- Customer has concerns about vendor stability
- Cultural fit is critical to the sale

**Q: Can customers negotiate the ethical clauses?**  
A: No. Sections 1.3 (Human Judgment Primacy) and 8.4 (Ethical Termination) are non-negotiable. We can negotiate pricing, SLAs, and technical terms, but not core principles.

**Q: What if a customer wants to remove the "no recommendations" language?**  
A: That's a disqualifier. Politely explain that if they need recommendations, ContinuuAI isn't the right fit. Offer to help them find alternative vendors.
